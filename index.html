About

I'm a mild-mannered senior at UCSD. I'm pursuing a major in Math-Computer Science and a minor in Math Education. Oh, did I say I was mild mannered? The only difference between me and Calvin is that I don't have an alter ego named Stupendous Man. And the stuffed tiger.

Every now and then, I like to come up with sarcastic or ironic things to say. But certainly not both.

Random Tidbits

https://aln.netlify.com/comics

This is my webcomic of random things. They're mostly geek-humor(ish) things that revolve around math, computer science, and the English language. The style and presentation are heavily inspired by Randall Munroe's xkcd. I only do these when — what I call — my "sarcastic switch" is triggered.

Projects

Because I have a minor in Math education, I'm interested in education. Or is it supposed to be the other way around? Either way, I'm interested in using technology to spread the availability of educational tools and resources. One of them was inspired by a high school teacher (for whom I apprenticed) who wanted a pi quiz that tested more digits. Two of them are study tools that were created out of my dislike of textbooks that assume the reader has strong background knowledge.

https://aln.netlify.com/projects

I'm also interested in mobile development for Android and iOS. I taught myself Android programming with several of Udacity's free Android courses. From there, I made several small apps that I (and probably only I) use to help make my life a little bit easier. I learned iOS programming while serving as an Instructor Aide for Program Your Future. I'll (probably won't) be the first to tell you that iOS programming is frustrating without any actual iOS devices.
